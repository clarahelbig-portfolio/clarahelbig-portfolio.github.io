New Perspectives (2018)

Jean Epstein speaks about Cinemaâ€™s ability to magnify life. With the Close-Up, he argues, the true greatness of human existence is made visible. He situates cinema and moving image practice as a form of artistic expression unbound by time and space, emphasising its unique ability to bend and manipulate it in more profound ways than any other medium. In his writing on Magnification, Epstein plays on the subjectivity and relativity of our experience.

Recreating the imagined perception of an insect, New Perspectives takes up Jean Epstein's ideas about the close up and transfers them onto fruit.

The film provides a playful comment on the often forgotten connectivity between species defining our anthropocentric world.
